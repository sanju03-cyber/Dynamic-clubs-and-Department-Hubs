Government Scholarship Schemes

S.No Scholarship Name Scholarship Details (Overview) Eligibility Criteria
1 National Merit Scholarship This scheme supports highly meritorious students by offering financial assistance for higher studies in colleges and universities. It reduces education expenses and encourages students to continue their academic journey. 80% and above in 10th/12th; must be enrolled in UG or Diploma courses; family income below government norms.
2 Post-Matric Scholarship (SC/ST/OBC) Financial support for students belonging to SC/ST/OBC categories to pursue post-matric education such as ITI, Diploma, UG, PG, and professional courses. Must belong to SC/ST/OBC; income limit between ₹1–2.5 lakh depending on category; must be studying in Class 11 to PG level.
3 Chief Minister’s Scholarship Scheme State-funded scheme to support economically backward students in professional courses, engineering, arts, and science courses offered by state institutes. State domicile certificate required; minimum 60% in previous exams; income below ₹2 lakh/year.
4 Minority Scholarship (Pre/Post Matric) This scholarship supports students belonging to Muslim, Christian, Sikh, Jain, Buddhist, and Parsi communities by covering tuition fees, books, and hostel charges. Must belong to a minority community; minimum 50% marks; family income less than ₹2.5 lakh/year.
5 National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship Provides financial assistance to students from weaker sections to prevent them from dropping out after Class 8 and encourages them to continue education up to Class 12. Studying in Class 9–12; must pass NMMS exam; family income below ₹1.5 lakh/year.
6 State Government Scholarship (Domicile) Each state offers multiple scholarships such as fee reimbursements, girl-student incentive schemes, and merit scholarships for different education levels. State domicile; marks criteria vary; income limit usually up to ₹2 lakh/year.
7 Central Sector Scholarship Scheme Provided by the Central Government to top-performing students who scored above 75% in their board exams. Helps with tuition fees for UG/PG studies. 75% or above in Class 12; studying in recognized college; income limit ₹6 lakh/year.
8 AICTE Pragati Scholarship for Girls Scholarship for girl students admitted to technical/engineering courses. Covers tuition fee reimbursement and provides additional allowances. Girl student; admitted to AICTE-approved institute; family income below ₹8 lakh/year.
9 AICTE Saksham Scholarship for Specially-Abled Students Supports students with disabilities pursuing technical or professional courses by covering education costs and providing allowances. Minimum 40% disability; admitted to AICTE-approved institute; income below ₹8 lakh/year.
10 PM Scholarship Scheme (PMSS) Supports wards of ex-servicemen, police, and defense personnel. Financial aid is given monthly for higher education, professional courses, and technical studies. Must be a ward of defense personnel/ex-servicemen; minimum 60% marks; studying in professional/technical courses.
